Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

1876-1948

Founder of Pakistan

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was the founder of Pakistan and one of South Asia’s most influential political leaders. Born on 25 December 1876 in Karachi, he was a trained lawyer who believed deeply in constitutionalism, justice, and the rule of law. Through his leadership of the All-India Muslim League, Jinnah united the Muslims of the subcontinent and articulated the Two-Nation Theory, which became the foundation of the Pakistan Movement. His political vision and determination led to the creation of Pakistan on 14 August 1947. After independence, he served as Pakistan’s first Governor-General and focused on building a democratic state based on equality, religious freedom, and national unity. Remembered as the Father of the Nation, Quaid-e-Azam’s principles — Unity, Faith, and Discipline — continue to guide Pakistan today.

Biographies

  • Studied law at Lincoln’s Inn, London
  • Initially joined the Indian National Congress
  • Later became the leader of the All-India Muslim League
  • Played a key role in the creation of Pakistan
  • Served as Pakistan's first Governor-General
  • Fought for constitutional rights and political representation of Muslims
  • Known for his principles of Unity, Faith, and Discipline
  • Advocated the Two-Nation Theory, stating that Muslims and Hindus were separate nations
  • Played a decisive role in the Lahore Resolution (1940)
  • Passed away on 11 September 1948
